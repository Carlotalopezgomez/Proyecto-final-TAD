Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley - Frankenstein

by Carlota López Gómez

Welcome to the mini digital edition of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein. Made as a project for Text as Data II, taught at the University of Antwerp.

Frankenstein, by Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley, has been one of the most influential Gothic novels since its publication in 1818. Thanks to the Shelley Godwin Archive and the Bodleian Library, the original manuscript of this novel is now accesible to the public. This project offers transcriptions of pages 75 to 84, corresponding to Chapter 7th of the novel, based on the information made available by the archive. The edition visualises additions, deletions and revisions made by both Mary Shelley and Percy Bysshe Shelley. These textual aspects have been encoded following the guidelines of the BDMP Encoding Manual.

This edition contains the following transcriptions:

The legacy of Mary Shelley's novel can be traced until modern days, remaining one of the most frequently adapted and reimagined narratives across different disciplines, incliding cinema or theatre. Frankenstein has evolved into a myth, probably one of the few with a documented author and precise date of origin. However, cinematographic adaptations of this novel have often spread a somehow disorted perception of both the story and its characters. Therefore, a visit to the original manuscript is worthwhile, in order to admire the beginning of the myth.
